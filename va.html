Our project in Visual Arts is integrated with Social Studies. Our task was to create a contemporary sculpture in relation to different communities and peace building. Students are tasked to show who they are in a community and how it relates to peace building by creating a message that could be interpreted through the artwork.

My project is someone doing a backhand spring on the beam. The community I chose was my gym, and I think it shows in my project that a lot of people are watching you, and if you make a mistake, many people will notice. Unity and harmony play a big part in showing the message in contemporary art because the shapes and colors express a lot and help you understand the project better. I feel like art has changed so much over the years. Comparing the Mona Lisa to pop art, it looks very different. I think now there are a lot more styles of art compared to before, where most felt more realistic.

This artwork shows a hand coming out of the ground. The hand is holding a pencil while ribbons tie it, trying to pull it back down. The pencil represents me in the art community, the hand represents me, and the ribbon represents me holding myself back.
